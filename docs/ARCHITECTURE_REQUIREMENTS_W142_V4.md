## 2. The requirement W142 adds

### R7′ — The architecture must DISCOVER a low-`p` technique from its own rare verified-correct sample and AMORTIZE it across a same-technique family without oracle leakage, gated by a per-family supply/discoverability estimator distinct from the per-item difficulty estimator (R4′).

**V3 (R6′) said:** the net needs a teaching-compiler head that lowers a verifier diagnostic into a leakage-audited teaching object (technique + holed skeleton + oracle routing), gated by a per-ability tutor-usability estimate.

**W141/W142 sharpen:** a teaching object is only worth compiling where the technique is *needed-and-rare* — present in the generator's distribution often enough to be DISCOVERED (`p>0`) but rare enough that per-problem resampling/verified-selection does not already reach it (`p` not saturated). That is a **family-level** property, and exploiting it is an **amortization**: discover the technique ONCE (pay the rare-discovery cost on one member, possibly with a raised discover budget), compile the scaffold, then AMORTIZE it across all same-technique members at the standard per-member budget. At equal total family budget the per-member superiority over no-oracle verified-selection is `(1−p)^{K_a}` — retirement-grade for moderate `p`, and exactly zero at the bimodal extremes (`p≈0` no discoverable sample; `p≈1` selection already saturates). So the architecture needs, beyond R4′'s per-*item* difficulty estimator, a **per-*family* supply/discoverability estimator** that (a) detects the needed-and-rare band, (b) decides whether to spend a raised discover budget, and (c) routes the discovered scaffold to the family's other members — all while a leakage gate guarantees the scaffold carries the *technique* (a gated decision the sub-module must fill), never the *answer*.

**Evidence (primary, wired):**
- **DreamCoder (arXiv:2006.08381, PLDI'21) / Voyager (arXiv:2305.16291, NeurIPS'23)** — library/skill learning that extracts a reusable abstraction from solved instances and amortizes it across a task family; closest true discover-once-amortize-many prior art, but both consume **oracle task specs / ground-truth I/O**. ⇒ R7′ must achieve the same amortization with a **no-oracle self-verifier** (the W141 byte-identical-to-grader verifier), not a ground-truth spec.
- **RLAD (arXiv:2510.02263)** — generates reasoning abstractions and shows test-time compute on abstractions beats more solution samples, but the abstractions are **per-problem** and the training abstractions are **distilled from a stronger oracle model**. ⇒ a CONTRAST that defines R7′'s white space: **per-family amortization** (not per-problem) and **self-derived** (not stronger-oracle-distilled).
- **ReST / ReST-EM (arXiv:2308.08998 / arXiv:2312.06585, TMLR'24)** — self-improvement from the model's own correct samples filtered by a binary verifier, amortizing one Grow over many Improve steps — but via **weight updates**. ⇒ R7′ is the **frozen-weight, inference-time** realization (a reused scaffold), which is also the only option for an API-only frontier model; the no-oracle verifier is the ReST-EM "binary verifier" made byte-identical to the hidden grader, with the documented leakage modes (test-reward leakage, verifier-overfitting) closed by the leak gate + the 0-FP/FN audit.
- **Self-Debug with self-generated tests (arXiv:2501.12793)** — predicting test *outputs* is the failure point (only 44–59% of self-test suites fully correct; trusting them costs up to −7pt). ⇒ R7′'s discover-verifier must **execute a self-written brute on self-generated small inputs** (compute outputs, never predict them) — the load-bearing W141 S1 design, kept as an architectural invariant with the false-positive rate gated.
- **metabench (arXiv:2407.12844, ICLR'25) / Fluid Benchmarking (arXiv:2509.11106, COLM'25)** — Fisher information peaks at `p≈0.5` and vanishes at `p∈{0,1}`; item value is capability-(θ-)dependent. ⇒ the per-family supply estimator is the IRT peak-information selector lifted to the *technique-family* granularity and conditioned on the generator's own ability — the formal basis for the moderate-`p` band and for why the bimodal halves carry no amortization headroom.

**Honest flag.** That ≥3 technique-distinct families live in the needed-and-rare band at a given model scale is the W142 EMPIRICAL question; the architectural requirement is only that the *capacity to estimate per-family supply, spend a discover budget, and amortize a leak-audited self-derived scaffold* is a precondition for converting a single coordination win into a family-spanning one. A thin band does not refute R7′ — it sharpens the supply-estimator sub-requirement (the net must know when NOT to spend the discover budget) and localises the blocker to the model's intrinsic bimodality.

**Architectural implication.** Beyond R6′'s teaching-compiler and R4′'s per-item difficulty estimator, the net needs (a) a **per-family supply/discoverability head** (is this technique needed-and-rare for *this* generator?), (b) a **discover-budget controller** (spend a raised discover budget once per family iff supply is in-band and the amortization horizon `M` is large enough that the per-member overhead `K_d/M` is negligible), and (c) a **scaffold-amortization router** that applies the one self-derived, leak-audited scaffold to the family's other members at the standard per-member budget — with an explicit equal-total-budget accounting so a raised discover budget is never silently folded into a per-problem same-budget claim.

## 3. What W142 specifically tests of R7′

W142 is the first probe of **family-supply convertibility**: *given the W141 self-derived no-oracle teaching object, do ≥3 technique-distinct, leak-audited-EXTRACTABLE families live in the moderate-`p` band `[0.10,0.50]` (fair neutral baseline, Wilson-95% excluding 0 and 1) such that discover-once-amortize-many beats no-oracle verified-selection by a retirement-grade margin at equal total family budget, spanning ≥3 families OR ≥2 modes, with a fake-scaffold control failing?* A PASS is the programme's first resistant multi-agent retirement and direct evidence R7′ is architecturally on-track. A FAIL localises the residual blocker as either **extraction reach** (the `acc += <gated-expr>` template covers counting/two-deque but not prefix-hash/BSoA — a teaching-compiler-representation limit, R6′) or **intrinsic family supply** (the frontier 70B's bimodal generation leaves <3 technique-distinct families in the band — a property of the model the supply estimator can *detect* but cannot *manufacture*).

## 4. Empirical result (W142)

_See `docs/RESULTS_W142_MODERATE_P_SCREEN_V1.md` for the per-family screen table + (if the family span earns) the Lane β pilot._ The $0 extractability gate result is locked independent of any NIM: the self-tutoring teaching-object representation (a single printed accumulator updated by `acc += <expr>` gated by an `if`/shrink-`while`) ADMITS the counting-pair (sort+two-pointer) and two-deque sliding-window veins and REJECTS the prefix-hash (technique in dict-maintenance, no gating predicate) and binary-search-on-answer (printed answer is a reassignment, not an accumulator) veins. So R6′'s teaching-compiler *representation* is itself a binding constraint on which families R7′ can amortize — a first-class architectural finding: **the discover-then-amortize head and the teaching-compiler head are coupled — a family is amortizable only if its technique is expressible as a gated-accumulator hole.** The fair-`p` band measurement + the family-span earn/cap are reported in the results doc and the COO-9 sync.
