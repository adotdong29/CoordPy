# Architecture Requirements — W140 V3 (refines V2)

## 1. Framing

This refines `docs/ARCHITECTURE_REQUIREMENTS_W139_V2.md` from the W140 survivors. It remains a **requirements document derived from empirical survivors**, not a claim that a coordination-native architecture exists. The long-term goal is unchanged: *a coordination-native neural architecture that internalizes memory, routing, verification, iterative feedback, and auditable coordination, and outperforms plain transformers on hard, contamination-resistant tasks at matched compute.* W139 closed the **cross-tier robustness** precondition of the verifier-routing triad (R2′ verifier matched to generator capability / R4′ per-ability difficulty / R5′ capability-aware allocation) but localised the residual blocker as the **ladder composition**: the witness gain stayed anchor-concentrated because the verifier's output was not actionable by weaker generators (the 8B's witness-usability was 0.00 — it could not convert "your algorithm is too slow" into a correct rewrite). W140 attacks exactly that, and adds one requirement: **R6′ — cross-capability teaching-compilation.** V2's R2′/R4′/R5′ and V1's R1, R3, R6–R12 stand unchanged. Each requirement still traces to specific milestones + primary sources; none is generic ML advice.

## 2. The requirement W140 adds

### R6′ — A verifier signal a weak sub-module cannot act on must be COMPILED into a sub-module-consumable TEACHING object, not merely suppressed; and the architecture needs a *teaching-compiler* head, gated by a per-ability tutor-usability estimate.

**V2 (R2′/R5′) said:** when the capability gate is below threshold, KEEP the base policy — *suppress* the verifier the generator cannot act on (the W139 non-negativity fix). That makes a strong-module verifier *safe* for weak sub-modules, but *useless* to them — the coordination gain stays concentrated at the one module that can both reach the informative band and act on the verifier.

**W140 sharpens:** suppression is a floor, not a ceiling. The architecture must be able to *lower* a strong-module diagnostic into a form a weaker sub-module can consume — a **family-level teaching object** that carries the *named technique* (the structural alternative to the naive method) plus a worked, hole-bearing scaffold and the execution-oracle routing — compiled from train-split / owned-oracle structure, not from the target answer. The routing decision is gated by a **per-ability tutor-usability estimate** (how often one tutor-conditioned attempt converts a failing candidate to passing for *this* sub-module), the teaching analogue of R4′'s per-ability difficulty estimate. Where the tutor lifts usability above threshold, APPLY the compiled tutor; where it does not, KEEP (R5′ preserved). The teaching-compiler is what can turn cross-tier *safety* (R2′/R5′) into cross-tier *usefulness*.

**Evidence (primary, wired):**
- The weak sub-module *can refine but cannot verify* (arXiv:2404.17140 ACL'24) ⇒ the compiled tutor must carry the execution-oracle routing, not just the technique name.
- Principle-only explanations *mislead* weak learners; a two-layer template (high-level + detailed scaffold) lifts a 7B (arXiv:2404.02213 CHI'24; arXiv:2410.09008 ICLR'25) ⇒ the teaching object is two-layer (name + holed skeleton), not a bare diagnostic.
- The winning self-correction mechanism *differs by capability* and a strong-tier curriculum can *harm* a weak one (arXiv:2503.08681; arXiv:2510.06101) ⇒ the teaching-compiler must be *capability-matched and gated*, and the per-ability tutor-usability estimate is the gate; a globally-applied strong-module explanation mis-serves weaker modules.
- CoT distillation can drop a student below its own baseline (arXiv:2604.08880) ⇒ the gate's reference is the sub-module's OWN baseline (the non-regression floor R5′ preserves).
- Reasoning abstractions as a separate generated object beat more solution samples (arXiv:2510.02263 RLAD) ⇒ a dedicated teaching-compiler head (abstraction-generator) is the right architectural locus, distinct from the solution-generator.

**Honest flag.** That a family-level teaching object can lift a weak generator where the raw verifier cannot is the W140 EMPIRICAL question; the architectural requirement is that the *capacity to compile and gate such an object* is a precondition for cross-tier coordination usefulness — supported by the literature, measured (not assumed) by W140.

**Architectural implication.** Beyond R2′'s capability gate and R5′'s KEEP/APPLY router, the net needs a **teaching-compiler module** that (a) reads the per-ability difficulty (R4′) and a per-ability *tutor-usability* estimate, (b) lowers a strong-module verifier diagnostic into a family-level, leakage-audited teaching object (technique + scaffold + oracle routing) when the sub-module is tutor-eligible, (c) KEEPs the base policy otherwise — all at matched compute. The teaching object must be *auditable not-the-answer* (the discriminating logic remains a hole the sub-module must fill); a teaching-compiler without a leakage gate is a contamination liability.

## 3. What W140 specifically tests of R6′

W140 is the first probe of **cross-tier USEFULNESS** (not just safety) of the verifier-routing triad: *given a per-ability band (R4′) and a capability gate (R2′/R5′), can a teaching-compiler (R6′) lower the anchor's winning verifier into a family-level tutor that lifts a WEAKER tier above its own self-consistency baseline by ≥+5pp, spanning ≥2 shared families, while staying non-negative vs blind reflexion on every tier?* A PASS is evidence the teaching-compiler is architecturally on-track for a coordination-native net whose strong modules must *teach* weaker sub-modules, not merely avoid harming them; a FAIL localises whether the residual blocker is the teaching object's *content* (R6′ compilation), the *gate* (R2′ tutor-usability estimate), or a genuine *capability wall* in the weak module (no teaching object can manufacture an absent sub-skill).

## 4. Empirical result (W140) — R6′ SUPPORTED (consumability + a same-budget weak-tier lift in the A1≈0 regime)

R6′ has two halves: (a) the compiled teaching object is *consumable* by weaker modules, and (b) consumability converts into a same-budget *usefulness* lift. W140 SUPPORTS BOTH, with a sharp regime condition:

- **(a) Consumability — established.** The tutor-usability calibration lifts the per-ability tutor-usability above τ=0.34 on every measured tier/family (8B 0.60/0.75, mid 1.00, strong 1.00) where W139's raw-witness usability was 0.00/0.00. The 8B's failure was a TEACHING gap, not a capability wall.
- **(b) Same-budget usefulness — SUPPORTED in the A1≈0 regime.** The decisive condition is the **substitute-vs-additive regime**: where the weak module's plain distribution ALREADY has mass on the technique (A1 high), same-budget self-consistency reaches the answer without the tutor, so the teaching gain is *substitutive* (the W140 dev: T2 ties A1 on easy-for-8B families). But where the technique is **needed-and-unknown (A1≈0)**, the tutor's distribution-shift is *additive* — it supplies a capability resampling lacks. The iter-2 probe confirms this: on `subarrays_sum_and_range@30000` (8B A1=25%, correct brute TLEs) the holed-skeleton tutor lifts the 8B to 100% (**+75pp** over same-budget A1); on `sum_nearest_smaller_left@50000` (8B A1=0%, monotonic-stack unknown) it lifts 0→100% (**+100pp**) — a same-budget weak-module lift spanning ≥2 families, far above the +5pp bar.
- **Architectural sharpening of R6′ (new, from W140).** The teaching-compiler is *additive* only in the **needed-and-unknown** regime; the routing gate must therefore condition not only on the per-ability tutor-usability (does the module *consume* it) but on the **per-ability self-consistency baseline** (does the module already *reach* it by resampling) — APPLY the compiled tutor where A1 is low AND tutor-usability is high; KEEP where A1 already captures the headroom. And the APPLY policy should be **always-apply the scaffold** in the A1≈0 regime, NOT a public-signal revert controller: W140 found the W139-style controller (`T4`) under-performs the always-apply scaffold (`T2`) because its revert is gated on public-sample pass count, which does not expose a HIDDEN_EDGE / TLE failure (the W125/W129 public-signal-non-discriminating limit) — so it reverts away from correct tutored code. The internal verifier that gates KEEP/APPLY must see the *hidden* failure (an owned-oracle / held-out signal), not the public samples.
