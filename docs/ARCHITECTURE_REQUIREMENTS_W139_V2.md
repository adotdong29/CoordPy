# Architecture Requirements — W139 V2 (refines V1)

## 1. Framing

This refines `docs/ARCHITECTURE_REQUIREMENTS_W138_V1.md` from the W139 survivors. It remains a **requirements document derived from empirical survivors**, not a claim that a coordination-native architecture exists. The long-term goal is unchanged: *a coordination-native neural architecture that internalizes memory, routing, verification, iterative feedback, and auditable coordination, and outperforms plain transformers on hard, contamination-resistant tasks at matched compute.* W139 closes one more precondition — **cross-tier robustness of the verifier-routing mechanism** — and the requirements it sharpens are R2 (verifier matched to generator capability), R4 (per-ABILITY in-band difficulty estimator), and R5 (capability-aware allocation). V1's R1, R3, R6–R12 stand unchanged. Each requirement still traces to specific milestones + primary sources; none is generic ML advice.

## 2. The three requirements W139 sharpens

### R2′ — The internal verifier must be matched to the GENERATOR's capability; a verifier the generator cannot act on must be suppressed, not applied.
**V1 said:** the architecture needs an internal verifier *stronger than* the generator. **W139 sharpens:** strength is necessary but not sufficient — the verifier's *output must be actionable by the generator*. W138 showed the exact-oracle complexity witness (a maximally strong verifier) HURTS an 8B by −25pp: the witness correctly diagnoses "your algorithm is too slow," but the 8B cannot implement the efficient algorithm, so it writes broken efficient code *below its own self-consistency baseline*. A strong verifier applied to a generator that cannot act on it is net-negative.
**Evidence.** W138 (8B C0−A1 = −25; anchor C0−A1 = +40 — same witness, opposite sign by tier). Primary: *Small Language Models Need Strong Verifiers to Self-Correct Reasoning* (arXiv:2404.17140) — small models gain from a strong EXTERNAL verifier but a weak self-verifier limits the gain; *LLMs Cannot Self-Correct Reasoning Yet* (arXiv:2310.01798) — signal-free revision can degrade. **Honest flag:** that the gap is *capability*-driven (bigger models act on the witness more) is our W138/W139 empirical finding, not a literature result — no verified primary abstract reports execution-feedback repair scaling with model size.
**Architectural implication.** The verifier head must be paired with a **capability gate**: a measured, per-(generator, problem) signal of whether the generator can convert the verifier's output into an improved candidate (W139 measures it as the *witness-usability rate* — how often one witness-reflexion attempt flips a secret-failing plain candidate to secret-passing). When the gate is below threshold the architecture must KEEP the base policy (fall back to plain self-consistency), never APPLY the verifier. A verifier without a capability gate is a liability on weak sub-modules.

### R4′ — The in-band difficulty estimator must be PER-ABILITY (per-model), not global.
**V1 said:** the architecture needs an internal difficulty estimator and must act only in the informative band p∈(0,1). **W139 sharpens:** the informative band is *capability-relative* — the same item is saturated for a strong tier and dead for a weak tier; a single global band is wrong for every model but one. W138 calibrated ONE band at the anchor and tested all tiers on it, so `llama-3.1-70b` sat at A1≈70% (near-saturated, off its own band) and the mechanism could only tie reflexion. The fix is a separate band per tier (each model tested at its own p≈0.5).
**Evidence.** W138 (mid tier near-saturated on the anchor band ⇒ C0−B0 = +0); W139 Lane α (per-tier recalibration). Primary: *Fluid Language Model Benchmarking* (arXiv:2509.11106) — "the relative value of benchmark items depends on an LM's capability level, suggesting that evaluation should adapt to each LM," selecting items dynamically like computerized adaptive testing; metabench (arXiv:2407.12844) / tinyBenchmarks (arXiv:2402.14992) for the IRT difficulty/discrimination basis; self-consistency is inert off-band (arXiv:2203.11171).
**Architectural implication.** The difficulty/uncertainty estimator must condition on the generator's *own* ability θ (peak Fisher information at that θ), producing a per-ability band; the coordination machinery activates only where the item is informative *for this generator*. A globally-fixed difficulty threshold mis-gates every model whose ability differs from the calibration model.

### R5′ — Capability-aware allocation must route the verifier CHANNEL ON only where the generator can use it, and KEEP elsewhere — at matched compute.
**V1 said:** spend the fixed compute adaptively across the difficulty band. **W139 sharpens:** the allocation lever includes *which feedback channel* (verifier-witness vs plain resample), routed by R2′'s capability gate, and the routing must guarantee the verifier never lowers a sub-policy below its own baseline. The W139 capability-matched controller is exactly this: same-budget K, KEEP=plain on incapable tiers (so the arm ≡ self-consistency, non-negative by construction), APPLY-with-per-problem-revert on capable tiers.
**Evidence.** W139 Lane β (the capability-matched controller); W125–W130 (uniform same-budget compute on a mis-gated field wastes budget). Primary: *Scaling LLM Test-Time Compute Optimally* (arXiv:2408.03314) — effectiveness of test-time-compute strategies varies with prompt difficulty, so allocation must be difficulty-(and thus capability-)conditioned; *Ensuring Functional Correctness ... Selective Generation* (arXiv:2505.13553) and *DOCE* (arXiv:2408.13745) — public/trial-test pass is a sound, risk-controllable commit/abstain signal; *Large Language Monkeys* (arXiv:2407.21787) — without a verifier, selection plateaus.
**Architectural implication.** A routing/allocation module that (a) reads the per-ability difficulty estimate (R4′) and the capability gate (R2′), (b) commits a verifier-revised candidate only when it clears a public/owned-oracle gate, and (c) otherwise reverts to the base policy — all at fixed total compute. Coordination superiority must hold across the model ladder, not at one tier; the allocation policy is what makes a tier-specific verifier gain *cross-tier robust*.

## 3. What W139 specifically tests of these requirements

W139 is the first probe of the **cross-tier robustness** of the verifier-routing triad (R2′ + R4′ + R5′): *given a per-ability band (R4′), can a capability-matched controller (R2′ + R5′) make a same-budget verifier gain hold POSITIVE on ≥2 model tiers (one being the frontier anchor) and NON-NEGATIVE on all tiers?* A PASS is evidence the triad is architecturally on-track for a coordination-native net that must serve sub-modules of differing capability; a FAIL localises whether the residual blocker is the per-ability band (R4′), the capability gate (R2′), or the allocation policy (R5′). W139 also revives the multi-MODE span (V1 R9) by repairing the W138 large-input firing limit of the counterexample witness (a probe-budget fix, `LARGE_PROBE_TOKEN_CAP`), so the verifier-routing is tested across ≥2 fixable modes, not one.

## 4. Empirical result (W139)

W139 SUPPORTS R2′ + R5′ and is a textbook case for R4′, but does NOT clear the cross-tier earn (see `docs/RESULTS_W139_CROSS_TIER_V1.md`):

- **R4′ (per-ability difficulty) — supported, sharply.** Per-tier recalibration found `llama-3.1-70b` (the "mid" tier) **SATURATED on the complexity family** (a1=1.00) — it is *stronger than the anchor* on this field, so a globally-anchor-calibrated band (W138) mis-gated it as "near-saturated with +0." A separate per-ability band is necessary and exposes that this tier simply has no complexity headroom.
- **R2′ + R5′ (capability-matched verifier + allocation) — supported.** The capability-matched controller routed APPLY only on the witness-eligible anchor and KEEP on the ineligible 8B + mid. Result: **non-negativity on every tier** (`Cm−A1 ≥ 0`), eliminating the W138 8B −25 harm AND protecting the 8B from blind reflexion's harm (B0 took the 8B from 25%→0%; Cm held 25%). A verifier the generator cannot act on is suppressed, not applied — exactly R2′/R5′.
- **What it does NOT establish.** §7a SPAN_FAIL (the witness's rescues over strong blind reflexion span 1 mode at the anchor) ⇒ no frontier earn. And no second tier on this 3-model ladder has BOTH headroom AND witness-usability (mid saturated, 8B capability-bound), so the gain stays anchor-concentrated. The apparent second positive tier was KEEP-tier sampling noise (n=2). ⇒ the residual blocker is NOT R2′/R4′/R5′ (those held) but the **ladder composition**: cross-tier robustness needs ≥2 tiers each in the informative band AND each capable of acting on the verifier — a property of the model set, which R4′ can detect but cannot manufacture. This motivates a W140 with a denser/different ladder.
